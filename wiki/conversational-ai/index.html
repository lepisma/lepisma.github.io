Conversational AI

≡ index / conversational-ai

From around 2018 to 2024 I worked on telephonic conversational voicebots. We focused on building speech-first systems and thus covered the full spectrum of ML from Speech to Text, SLU, Dialog Management, and back to speech via Speech Synthesis.

This page hosts a few notes around Conversational Voicebots that I think could be helpful for people working on similar systems.

1. Readings & Resources

While the technology powering the models have changed rapidly, a few core ideas have stayed the same. Here are a few introductory readings for the area that might be helpful even if they are not completely up to date:

  1. Designing Voice User Interfaces by Cathy Pearl is something we used to recommend to everyone joining teams that work on Conversational AI. There might be more extensive and modern resources now, but this book gets you introduced very quickly at the right level of abstraction.
  2. Spoken Language Understanding: Systems for Extracting Semantic Information from Speech by Tur and Mori is a dated book but it will open you up to understanding what all levers you could employ if you are stuck in a production situation. This is more technical so probably not very useful for people outside the ML or Engineering teams.
  3. In case you want a more Pop-Science treatment of the history of voice tech, check out The Voice in the Machine by Pieraccini.

I also recommend spending time on the following directions for people on the ground:

  1. Digital Audio Representation. To ensure you don't get stuck because of unawareness of encoding decoding involved somewhere in your product. You can pick videos like this as good starting points.
  2. Task Oriented Dialog (TOD) systems. Most production systems are called Task or Goal Oriented Dialog Systems. They are different than open systems like ChatGPT where you can discuss anything in any way. Knowing the difference here and various constraints and frameworks you can impose on the design and evaluation is a prioritization superpower.
  3. A few more academic ideas like Paralinguistics and Pragmatics that show up on the fringes of conversational systems. While they don't directly contribute to your product performance, they give you good frameworks to deconstruct problems in conversations.
  4. In 2025, it seems like the best way to make production ready voicebots is standardized enough and you can use frameworks like pipecat or livekit (for more low-level control) and follow guides like this to get something out in production. You will still need to work on the assisting systems around the bot so as to report, monitor, understand, debug, and improve the bot after and before going live. Some fluency with MLOps concepts will be helpful to think about these properly.

For keeping up with the field in general here are a few conferences you should follow:

  1. Interspeech, probably the most important one for the field. If you can only cover one conference, cover this.
  2. ICASSP
  3. EMNLP
  4. ACL

Of course you should skim through the general ones like NeurIPS. Sometimes music and audio conferences like ISMIR, ADC, etc. are also fun to follow. If, like me, you don't like twittering noises, you can follow Daily Papers from huggingface which can provide feeds at weekly or monthly frequency. During my work we used to maintain readings in open lists below:

  1. Our group library on Zotero
  2. Seminars webpage
  3. Paper Reading

They might be too old to be useful but you might find a few interesting pieces from there.

For a more industrial side of this, there used to be a conference called SpeechTEK that's inactive now. From the content that I saw there, it was very helpful to find right use-cases, markets, people, and solutions to industry specific issues. I am not sure if there is an equivalent of this now a days.

Note that the recommendations above might be outdated and I am planning to do some work to find current best-practices (<2025-07-23 Wed>). Once I have done the updates, I will update this note. Specifically I see more books around Conversational AI, blogs by platform companies, courses, etc. But since I have not gone through them yet, I can't recommend them. You can find many of them by doing simple web/llm searches.
